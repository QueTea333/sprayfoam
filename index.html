Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about spray foam insulation

How much does spray foam insulation cost?

Spray foam insulation typically costs between $1.50-$3.50 per square foot for residential projects, depending on the type (open-cell vs closed-cell), thickness, and project size. We provide free, detailed quotes within 24 hours that include a complete breakdown of costs and projected energy savings.

Is spray foam insulation worth the investment?

Absolutely! Most homeowners see 30-50% reduction in energy bills, with full ROI achieved in 3-7 years. Beyond energy savings, spray foam adds structural strength, prevents moisture issues, reduces noise, and increases home value. It's the only insulation with a lifetime warranty.

How long does spray foam installation take?

Most residential projects are completed in 1-3 days depending on size and complexity. Commercial projects vary based on building size. The foam cures within 24-48 hours, after which your space is fully usable.

Is spray foam insulation safe?

Yes! Once cured (24-48 hours), spray foam is completely safe, non-toxic, and won't off-gas harmful chemicals. It actually improves indoor air quality by sealing out pollutants, allergens, and preventing mold growth. We use high-quality materials and follow all safety protocols during installation.

What's the difference between open-cell and closed-cell spray foam?

Open-cell is lighter, less expensive, with an R-value of 3.5-3.7 per inch. It's ideal for interior walls and sound dampening. Closed-cell is denser with R-6 to R-7 per inch, acts as a vapor barrier, and provides structural support. It's perfect for exterior applications and areas requiring maximum insulation.
